# Synthesis Report

Combine research findings into a structured, actionable report.

## Steps

1. **Gather findings**: Collect all research outputs from previous deep-research runs or from the conversation context.

2. **Identify themes**: Group findings into major themes/categories.

3. **Assess consensus**: For each theme:
   - What do multiple sources agree on? (high confidence)
   - Where do sources disagree? (flag as contested)
   - What has only single-source support? (low confidence)

4. **Write the report**:

---

**Research Synthesis: [Topic]**
*[Date] | [N] sources analyzed | Overall confidence: [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW]*

**Executive Summary**
[3-5 sentences capturing the most important findings]

**Key Findings**
1. [Finding] — Confidence: HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW
   - Supporting evidence: [brief]
   - Sources: [N sources agree]

2. [Finding] — Confidence: HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW
   - Supporting evidence: [brief]
   - Sources: [N sources agree]

**Areas of Consensus**
- [What most sources agree on]

**Areas of Disagreement**
- [Where sources contradict] — Source A says X, Source B says Y

**Gaps and Limitations**
- [What we couldn't find]
- [Where data is insufficient]

**Recommended Next Research**
- [Specific follow-up questions that would increase confidence]

**Sources** (sorted by credibility)
1. [Source] — Credibility: X/10
2. [Source] — Credibility: X/10

---

## Important Rules

- Lead with the executive summary — it should stand alone
- Confidence ratings are mandatory for every finding
- Disagreements are MORE valuable than agreements — highlight them
- Always end with what you DON'T know and what research would help
- The report should be useful to someone who hasn't seen the raw research
